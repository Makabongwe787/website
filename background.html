Background & Personal Life

Understanding my roots, family, and the values that shaped me

Where I'm From

I was born and raised in Eswatini, a beautiful country in Southern Africa with a rich cultural heritage. My father's home is in Nhlangano, a meaningful place that connects me to my family's roots and cultural identity. Growing up in Eswatini shaped my worldview, taught me to appreciate my heritage, and instilled in me a sense of belonging to a community with shared values and traditions.

Eswatini is a country where community, respect, and cooperation are fundamental to society. These values, deeply woven into the fabric of my upbringing, continue to influence how I approach relationships, work, and my responsibilities as a student and future professional. My connection to Nhlangano and to Eswatini as a whole is an integral part of who I am.

Growing Up in an Extended Family

I grew up in an extended family environment, surrounded by many relatives—grandparents, cousins, aunts, and uncles. This wasn't a limitation but a blessing. Living in a household with multiple generations and perspectives taught me invaluable life lessons that shaped my character from an early age. In an extended family, you learn that life is about relationships, cooperation, and shared responsibility.

Growing up this way taught me the importance of respect—not just for elders, but for every person's contribution and perspective. I learned cooperation when sharing resources and responsibilities was necessary. I developed patience through navigating the dynamics of living with diverse personalities and preferences. Most importantly, I learned adaptability—the ability to adjust to different situations, understand various viewpoints, and find common ground with people from different backgrounds and ages.

These lessons transcend the domestic sphere. They directly inform how I approach teamwork in academic projects, collaborate with classmates, and envision working in professional environments. I understand that success is not individual—it is shared. This mindset has influenced how I view leadership, collaboration, and my role within communities.

My Siblings and Family Structure

I come from a family of five children: I am one of four siblings. Being part of a larger household with siblings taught me lessons that continue to shape my discipline, work ethic, and interpersonal skills. Growing up with siblings means learning early how to negotiate, share, and advocate for your needs while respecting the needs of others.

My siblings and I learned negotiation in its rawest form—managing shared spaces, dividing chores, and balancing individual desires with family needs. We learned to support each other through challenges and celebrate each other's victories. These experiences taught me resilience, patience, and the importance of standing my ground while also being willing to compromise for the greater good.

Surprisingly, these sibling dynamics translate remarkably well into group projects, problem-solving sessions, and real-world work environments. The same skills that helped me navigate family life—communication, empathy, problem-solving, and mutual support—are essential in professional collaborations. My siblings are my first teachers in teamwork, and that foundation has served me well as a student and will continue to be invaluable in my career.

Values Shaped by My Background

My upbringing in Eswatini, within an extended family, has given me a strong foundation of values that continue to guide me. These aren't abstract principles—they're lived experiences embedded in everything I do.

Respect

Growing up in a culture that deeply values respect for elders, family, and community, I learned that respect is the foundation of all meaningful relationships. This extends to respecting diverse perspectives, acknowledging different strengths, and valuing the contributions of every team member.

Cooperation

In an extended family, cooperation is necessary and natural. I learned that working together toward common goals is more powerful than individual effort. This value shapes how I approach collaborative projects and how I contribute to team environments—always seeking ways to work synergistically with others.

Responsibility

Being part of a large family meant having responsibilities that extended beyond myself. I understood early that my actions affect others and that taking ownership of my commitments is essential. This sense of responsibility extends to my academic work, my relationship with technology, and my vision for creating solutions that positively impact communities.

Adaptability

Living with multiple generations and diverse personalities required flexibility and the ability to adjust to different situations. As a Computer Science student, adaptability is critical—technology evolves constantly, and the ability to learn, adjust, and apply new knowledge is essential to success and growth.

Personal Growth & Development

My background—both geographic and familial—is not something I've moved past; it's something I carry forward. The values I learned, the perspectives I gained, and the resilience I developed are the foundation of who I am today and who I aspire to become.

These early life experiences continue to shape my discipline, work ethic, and interpersonal skills. They inform how I approach challenges, collaborate with peers, and envision contributing to organizations and communities in the future. I am proud of my roots, grateful for the lessons my family taught me, and committed to carrying those values forward as I grow as a technology professional and as a person.
